What Happened Today

The events that drove today's tape — a second semis-led rout, a fundamental AI-demand scare from SK Hynix, and the Micron print that sets tomorrow's memory open.

1. SK Hynix slows HBM4 to chase commodity DRAM — the day's fundamental catalyst The fresh fuel for the rout was not sentiment but a supply decision: SK Hynix said it is slowing advanced AI-chip (HBM4) production to redirect capacity into conventional DRAM, where acute shortages have made margins increasingly attractive (its 2026 HBM was already fully sold out). The market read the move two ways at once — bearish as a signal that AI-compute demand may be cresting (why pivot away from HBM if HBM demand were insatiable?), and bullish for commodity-DRAM pricing tightness. Today the tape weighted the demand-cresting read, and the memory complex was sold a second straight day.
2. Semis led the market lower again — Nasdaq ~-3.3%, S&P 500 ~-1.4%, Dow ~flat The selling stayed concentrated in semiconductors and high-multiple AI names: NVDA -4.2%, Qualcomm -8%, AMD -5.8%, SanDisk -11.2%, ARM weak, with Micron sliding further into its own after-hours print. Tesla -5.8% and Oracle -5.8% extended the high-beta unwind. Crucially, the Dow held roughly flat — capital rotated into healthcare and consumer-defensives, the classic late-cycle risk-off posture. That defensive-leadership / flat-Dow / Nasdaq-down split is the cleanest tell of the day: this is a rotation out of the AI trade, not (yet) an indiscriminate liquidation.
3. Micron's fiscal Q3 print — the decider — lands AMC into a maximally skeptic tape Micron reported fiscal third-quarter results after the close, the single most-watched read of the week. The setup: HBM capacity sold out through 2026, gross margin guided near 81%, consensus around $34.5-35B revenue and ~$19.7-20 EPS, with the market focused less on the headline than on HBM pricing into 2027 and the Q4 guide (consensus ~$38-40B). Quick Reference — Key Terms

GEX (Gamma Exposure)
Net dealer gamma position. Positive = stabilizing (sell rallies, buy dips); negative = amplifying. Approximated here qualitatively from VIX term structure since paid GEX feeds are not available.
VIX9D
9-day implied vol on SPX. Captures event windows tighter than spot VIX (30-day). VIX9D > spot VIX = front-end event premium.
Vanna
Sensitivity of option delta to IV. IV crush on benign news forces mechanical dealer buying.
Charm
Delta decay over time. Bleeds a call-heavy book even in a flat tape; runs 24/7 and accelerates into expiration.
Magnet / Accelerator / Trapdoor
Strike vocabulary. Magnet = positive-gamma strike attracting price. Accelerator = negative-gamma strike amplifying. Trapdoor = cluster that cascades.
Block tape
Institutional flow read. Approximated here via top movers since paid block-flow data is not available.
