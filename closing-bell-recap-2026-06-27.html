Quick Reference — Key Terms

GEX (Gamma Exposure)
Net dealer gamma position. Positive = stabilizing (sell rallies, buy dips); negative = amplifying. Approximated here qualitatively from VIX term structure since paid GEX feeds are not available.
VIX9D
9-day implied vol on SPX. Captures event windows tighter than spot VIX (30-day). VIX9D > spot VIX = front-end event premium.
Vanna
Sensitivity of option delta to IV. IV crush on benign news forces mechanical dealer buying.
Charm
Delta decay over time. Bleeds a call-heavy book even in a flat tape; runs 24/7 and accelerates into expiration.
Magnet / Accelerator / Trapdoor
Strike vocabulary. Magnet = positive-gamma strike attracting price. Accelerator = negative-gamma strike amplifying. Trapdoor = cluster that cascades.
Block tape
Institutional flow read. Approximated here via top movers since paid block-flow data is not available.

Bottom Line — Two Stories in One Session

The session story: The feared global AI de-rate was absorbed into the Friday close — the market refused to break. Stocks gapped down hard at the open (ES ~-0.5%, NQ ~-1.3%) on the NYT report that OpenAI is weighing pushing its IPO to 2027 and an overnight KOSPI 8% circuit breaker (SK Hynix/Samsung leading a regional memory rout, SoftBank ~-12%), then clawed nearly all of it back: the S&P closed -0.05% at 7,354.02, the Nasdaq Composite -0.24% at 25,297.62 (a 5th straight down day, but with losses cut hard), the Dow -0.09% at 51,876.11, and the Russell 2000 +0.71% as money kept rotating out of mega-cap AI. The week's split held into the bell: chips bled at the single-name level (SanDisk -10%, Marvell -5%, Micron -5% giving back its Thursday +17% pop, Arm -4%, Intel -3%, Nvidia -1% and on pace for ~-8% on the week), while the Mag-7 ex-NVDA/GOOGL closed green. The fear drained — the VIX eased to 18.89, the 10Y eased to ~4.4%, the dollar slipped to 101.25 off its 1-year high, WTI fell ~2.3% to ~$70 on Hormuz tanker exits, gold stayed heavy near $4,000, and bitcoin firmed near $60k. No major-cap reported after the bell (a Friday), so there was no AH-earnings cascade — the setup into Monday rests on a cash tape that absorbed the scare while the memory-supply thesis stayed confirmed by the week's Apple/MSFT price hikes. The book — the long-DTE AI/structural cohort (DRAM/APLD/SKM/RDW/NOK) — absorbed the chip beta but held full size, with NOK/RDW catching the rotation. Into the new week: quarter-end (6/30) rebalancing, a holiday-shortened stretch (markets closed Fri 7/3), and the June jobs report pulled forward to Thu 7/2. The under-the-hood story: The actual character of the close is a valuation/financing/positioning de-rate that the market absorbed, not a demand break it priced. The bears own three hooks — OpenAI's IPO/valuation doubt, the Apple/MSFT cost-pass-through optics, and the memory-cost-margin-squeeze worry — and the tape de-risked chips on them, but the gap-down was bought, the VIX eased, the dollar/rate de-escalated, and the broad market and the Russell held. None is a crack in memory demand: Micron is sold out, and Apple and Microsoft are raising prices precisely because memory is scarce and expensive, confirming supply-side pricing power even as it squeezes device-maker margins. The forward deciders are whether the chip de-risk finds a floor (SanDisk -10% the warning shot), Monday's Seoul cash session and the won, the dollar/10Y path into the pulled-forward jobs print, and quarter-end mechanics into thin holiday-week liquidity.
