What Happened Today

The events that set today's tape, and the sequence from here.

1. FIVE BANKS BEAT BIG — JPM REVENUE +27% Y/Y, PROFIT +41%, DIMON FLAGS RECORD REVENUE IN EVERY BUSINESS The demand-side circuit breaker fired green. JPMorgan posted Q2 revenue up ~27% y/y to ~$57B (vs ~$51B est) with profit up ~41% — one of its largest beats in recent memory — and Jamie Dimon said every major business posted record revenue. Wells Fargo earned $2.00 vs $1.72 est (net income $6.41B, revenue +9% to $22.62B, profit +17%). Bank of America printed $1.21 vs $1.13 (revenue +15% to $31.6B, profit +27%). Goldman Sachs and Citigroup rounded out the slate with strong equities-trading revenue across the group. Five institutions holding >$13T in combined assets delivered the most concentrated single-day demand signal of the year — and it was unambiguously strong.
2. JUNE CPI COOLED ON THE HEADLINE, STUCK ON THE CORE The 8:30 print resolved the backward-looking way the setup implied: June headline CPI came in soft on a ~10% monthly drop in gasoline (~4th-largest of the decade), pulling the annual rate toward ~3.8-3.9% from May's 4.2%. But core — the number the Fed actually watches — held sticky at ~2.9% y/y (~+0.2-0.3% m/m), refusing to break lower. The market read it correctly: a one-month energy dip built on June's pre-escalation oil is not a regime change, especially with Brent now back above $85. The reaction was muted, not relief-driven.
3. WARSH'S FIRST TESTIMONY LEANS HAWKISH — HIKE BIAS INTACT Fed Chair Kevin Warsh made his first congressional appearance (House Financial Services, 10 AM), fielding questions on inflation, the rate path, the balance sheet, and whether AI is inflationary. He gave no dovish tell and did not tip his hand on AI, leaving the market's September hike + January 2027 hike pricing intact. With sticky core in hand and oil re-spiking, a chair testifying live at 10 AM into a cool headline was the mechanism that kept equities from converting the bank beats into a breakout. He testifies before the Senate Wednesday.
4. OIL THE OVERHANG — US DECLARES ITSELF 'GUARDIAN OF THE STRAIT,' IMPOSES A 20% HORMUZ TOLL President Trump reinstated a shipping blockade / 20% toll on the Strait of Hormuz, with the US styling itself 'guardian of the Strait.' Brent crossed $85 a barrel (intraday ~$85.49) for the first time in a month; WTI ~$80.80. This is a policy mechanic, not a one-off headline: a formal toll on the world's most important oil chokepoint keeps a structural premium in the crude curve and re-arms the inflation-expectations channel — the 10Y climbed toward ~4.6% and the dollar firmed (DXY +0.31% to ~101.28).
5. CHIPS STABILIZED — THE MONDAY ROUT DID NOT EXTEND After Monday's -4.8% chip rout (MU -4.3%, NVDA -3.5%, SNDK -12.6%, AMD -4.2%, INTC -6.1%) dragged the Nasdaq -1.55%, the semis complex halted its selloff today and stabilized the index into a roughly flat close. Ericsson's Q2 added a subtle demand tell: it flagged rising memory and custom-chip prices from suppliers tied to the AI build-out — a buyer complaining about component-cost inflation is a pricing-power signal for the memory cycle. The rout paused; it has not reversed.

Quick Reference — Key Terms

GEX (Gamma Exposure)
Net dealer gamma position. Positive = stabilizing (sell rallies, buy dips); negative = amplifying. Approximated here qualitatively from VIX term structure since paid GEX feeds are not available.
VIX9D
9-day implied vol on SPX. Captures event windows tighter than spot VIX (30-day). VIX9D > spot VIX = front-end event premium.
Vanna
Sensitivity of option delta to IV. IV crush on benign news forces mechanical dealer buying.
Charm
Delta decay over time. Bleeds a call-heavy book even in a flat tape; runs 24/7 and accelerates into expiration.
Magnet / Accelerator / Trapdoor
Strike vocabulary. Magnet = positive-gamma strike attracting price. Accelerator = negative-gamma strike amplifying. Trapdoor = cluster that cascades.
Block tape
Institutional flow read. Approximated here via top movers since paid block-flow data is not available.
