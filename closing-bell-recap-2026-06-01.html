What Happened Today

June opened split: NVDA's Arm-based N1X reveal at Computex re-fired the AI-everywhere trade and lifted ARM/HPE/IBM toward a Nasdaq record, while the weekend Iran ceasefire collapse gapped oil ~5% higher and dragged the Dow and Russell red — a tech bid over a geopolitical risk-off, with VIX back near 16.

1. NVDA unveiled the N1X Arm-based PC processor at Computex — co-developed with Microsoft and MediaTek, integrating a Blackwell GPU — and the AI cohort ran: NVDA ~+5%, ARM jumped, HPE/HPQ ~+8% intraday, ServiceNow and IBM bid, Nasdaq toward records At Taiwan's Computex, Jensen Huang unveiled the N1X — Nvidia's first PC processor, an Arm-based CPU developed with MediaTek and paired with a Blackwell GPU, co-engineered with Microsoft and slated to ship in Windows laptops from major brands this fall. Huang framed it as 'reinventing the PC … as big of a deal as the reinvention of the phone into what we now know as the smartphone' and 'the first completely re-engineered, reinvented line of PCs in 40 years.' The market read it as a structural new TAM for the AI build-out: NVDA rose ~5%, ARM jumped on the direct Arm-architecture validation (the N1X CPU is Arm-based), HPE and HPQ climbed ~8% intraday, and ServiceNow and IBM caught the software/AI-PC bid. Intel and AMD slipped ~1% each on the new PC-CPU competition. For the book this is the highest-signal development of the session: it directly validates the ARM 240C royalty thesis, extends the AI demand vector from servers into the AI-PC refresh cycle (incremental DRAM/HBM and networking content), and broadens the cohort halo onto a fresh, non-earnings catalyst.
2. The weekend US-Iran ceasefire framework collapsed — US self-defense strikes on Iranian radar/drone sites, an IRGC retaliatory strike on an air base, Israel pushing troops into Lebanon, Kuwait reporting incoming fire — and oil gapped up (WTI ~+4-6%, Brent ~+3-5%) on Strait of Hormuz disruption risk Over the weekend the tentative 60-day ceasefire MoU that markets had been pricing toward unraveled. Per the Associated Press and Reuters, the US said it conducted 'self-defense strikes' on Iranian radar and drone sites after Iran shot down an American drone; Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps said its aerospace force then targeted an air base used for US attacks; Israel ordered troops to move further into Lebanon in its fight with Hezbollah; and Kuwait reported incoming fire. A US official said Washington had proposed a 'gradual de-escalation' plan, and Trump had said Friday he would 'soon' decide on the deal — but no agreement was reached. Oil gapped up to start the week: WTI roughly +4-6% toward $90-92, Brent +3-5% toward $94-95, on renewed Strait of Hormuz disruption risk (IG's Tony Sycamore: 'even if an agreement is reached, it won't deliver a flood of supply'). This is the bear-leg from Friday's risk matrix firing: it dragged the Dow and Russell 2000 red, lifted energy, pushed VIX back near 16, and is the source of the day's split character. It is a tailwind for RDW (defense budget) and SLV (safe-haven/inflation hedge) and a risk to the long-duration book through the yield channel.
3. Split tape: Nasdaq led toward records on the N1X bid while the Dow (~-0.2 to -0.3%) and Russell 2000 (~-0.6 to -0.9%) finished red as the oil spike hit the broad market; VIX ticked ~+4% to near 16 from Friday's 15.74 cycle low The index tape bifurcated cleanly along the day's two catalysts. Tech/AI led — the Nasdaq flirted with fresh records on the NVDA/ARM/HPE bid — while the broad market sagged under the oil spike and the geopolitical risk-off: the Dow fell roughly 0.2-0.3% and the Russell 2000 dropped ~0.6-0.9%, with the S&P 500 hovering essentially flat to slightly lower around the 7,580 area (intraday snapshots showed it dipping in and out of positive). VIX rose ~4% to near 16, off Friday's 15.74 cycle low, as oil-vol (OVX) and the energy complex bid. Breadth was narrow and tilted negative beneath the headline — the bid was concentrated in the AI-PC cohort, not broad participation, the mirror image of Friday's Dow-led narrow breadth. The structural read: the cohort halo is intact and now riding a fresh catalyst, but the tape is no longer a one-way rate-relief grind — the geopolitical premium is back in the mix and breadth is the thing to watch for whether the AI bid can broaden or stays a single-cohort story. ISM Manufacturing printed 54.0 for May — the highest since May 2022 (4-year high), New Orders 56.8, fifth straight month of expansion — confirming the Chicago-PMI manufacturing tailwind, though overshadowed by the oil move The ISM Manufacturing PMI registered 54.0% in May, up 1.3 points from April and the highest reading since May 2022 (55.9%), with the sector expanding for a fifth consecutive month. The New Orders Index expanded for a fifth straight month at 56.8% (up 2.7 points), the overall economy continued in expansion for a 19th month, and the print corresponds to roughly +2.2% annualized real GDP. Coming after Friday's Chicago PMI 62.7 (4-year high), it confirms that manufacturing-cohort expansion is real and broadening into US-domestic activity — a structural cross-check that the AI-infrastructure capex cycle is showing up in hard data. But on the day it was overshadowed by the oil/geopolitical move, and its second-order effect is double-edged for the book: a stronger economy plus higher oil firms the inflation/rate narrative, which is the transmission that pressures the long-duration legs even as it validates the demand backdrop.
Framework read: June opened as a split tape — the AI-everywhere thesis got its cleanest fresh catalyst of the cycle in the N1X reveal (an Arm-based PC chip that directly validates the ARM royalty trade and extends the build-out into AI-PCs), while the geopolitical premium that the framework flagged in Friday's risk matrix went live as the US-Iran ceasefire collapsed and oil gapped ~5%. The bull-combo path into Tuesday: ARM holds the N1X pop, HPE/CRDO AH prints confirm cohort-cousin demand, oil fades on a de-escalation headline, JOLTS lands in-line, and the long-duration book re-extends as the rate-relief tailwind returns. Quick Reference — Key Terms

GEX (Gamma Exposure)
Net dealer gamma position. Positive = stabilizing (sell rallies, buy dips); negative = amplifying. Approximated here qualitatively from VIX term structure since paid GEX feeds are not available.
VIX9D
9-day implied vol on SPX. Captures event windows tighter than spot VIX (30-day). VIX9D > spot VIX = front-end event premium.
Vanna
Sensitivity of option delta to IV. IV crush on benign news forces mechanical dealer buying.
Charm
Delta decay over time. Bleeds a call-heavy book even in a flat tape; runs 24/7 and accelerates into expiration.
Magnet / Accelerator / Trapdoor
Strike vocabulary. Magnet = positive-gamma strike attracting price. Accelerator = negative-gamma strike amplifying. Trapdoor = cluster that cascades.
Block tape
Institutional flow read. Approximated here via top movers since paid block-flow data is not available.
