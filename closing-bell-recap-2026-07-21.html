Quick Reference — Key Terms

GEX (Gamma Exposure)
Net dealer gamma position. Positive = stabilizing (sell rallies, buy dips); negative = amplifying. Approximated here qualitatively from VIX term structure since paid GEX feeds are not available.
VIX9D
9-day implied vol on SPX. Captures event windows tighter than spot VIX (30-day). VIX9D > spot VIX = front-end event premium.
Vanna
Sensitivity of option delta to IV. IV crush on benign news forces mechanical dealer buying.
Charm
Delta decay over time. Bleeds a call-heavy book even in a flat tape; runs 24/7 and accelerates into expiration.
Magnet / Accelerator / Trapdoor
Strike vocabulary. Magnet = positive-gamma strike attracting price. Accelerator = negative-gamma strike amplifying. Trapdoor = cluster that cascades.
Block tape
Institutional flow read. Approximated here via top movers since paid block-flow data is not available.

Bottom Line — Two Stories in One Session

The session story: A second straight chip-led up day that quietly took on a rate headwind. The S&P closed +0.89% at 7,509.20, the Nasdaq Composite +1.29% at 25,837.21 and the Dow +0.74% (+385 points) at 52,224.64, snapping a three-day losing run — led again by semis (Micron +12.2%, SanDisk +14.3%, AMD +8.1%, Intel +8.6% on DCAI job-cut plans, Nvidia +2%) on strong Taiwan and South Korea export data, and broadened by earnings beats in 3M (+7%) and GM (~+5%). VIX eased to 18.65. But the day carried a counter-current the green screen masked: oil re-surged on a 10th consecutive day of US-Iran strikes, driving the 10Y to a two-month high near 4.64% and the dollar higher. The market repaired most of the range and now has to defend it into Wednesday's Alphabet, Tesla and Texas Instruments prints.

The under-the-hood story: Under the hood, the rebound got better in character even as the macro got worse. Monday's bounce was narrow and covering-led; today's was broader and export-data-driven — Micron +12% on Taiwan/Korea export strength is a fundamental leg, not a short-squeeze signature, and 3M and GM joining says the tape will pay for genuine beats across sectors. That is the encouraging half. The discouraging half is that the same session put the 10Y at a two-month high on re-surging oil, an un-priced valuation headwind that a green equity screen conveniently hides and that lands first on the long-duration AI-infra names. The framework's read: the AI-capex question is still fully open beneath a recovered index, and the market has spent two sessions positioning for a favourable answer it cannot actually receive until Wednesday 4:00 PM — while the rate channel quietly reloaded.

Mechanically honest read: State what is known and what is not, in order of certainty. Known: S&P +0.89% (7,509.20), Nasdaq Composite +1.29% (25,837.21), Dow +0.74% (+385.38, 52,224.64), three-day losing run snapped; MU +12.2%, SanDisk +14.3%, AMD +8.1%, INTC +8.6% (DCAI job cuts), NVDA +2%; 3M +7%, GM ~+5%; VIX 18.65 (-0.6%); 10Y at a two-month high ~4.64% on re-surging oil and a 10th day of US-Iran strikes, dollar higher; COF +2.1% AH on a clean beat (adj EPS $5.81 vs $4.69, rev $15.9B, provision ~$3.0B vs ~$4.0B). Not known: whether the two-day rebound is durable or front-run, whether Alphabet defends its capex line, how TXN guides the analog/data-center cycle, whether Tesla sours the mega-cap tone, where the oil-and-rate tail runs into the FOMC, and where Nokia's quarter lands.
