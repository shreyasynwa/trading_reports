Overnight Developments

What changed into this open: a bounce attempt after a two-day, semis-led AI-valuation rout — with the two events that decide the next leg (Micron tonight, May PCE tomorrow) both still ahead.

1. Futures bounce after a two-day, $1T+ AI rout — bargain-hunters test the low. S&P 500 futures are up ~0.3% and Nasdaq 100 futures ~0.6% into the open as investors bargain-hunt after two consecutive technology-driven down days. Tuesday 6/23 was the deeper of the two: the S&P fell 1.44% to 7,365.46, the Nasdaq Composite dropped ~2.2% to ~25,587, and the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX) slid 7.9%. The two-day drawdown wiped more than $1 trillion of value off the Nasdaq-100. The bounce is genuine but unproven — whether breadth and TICK confirm the futures pop in the cash session, or it fades into the Micron print, is the first tell of the day.
2. The selloff's catalysts — a BofA rate-hike note plus AI-capex ROI doubts. Two threads drove the rout. First, a Bank of America research note portending up to three interest-rate hikes this year; traders are now pricing a second Fed hike by December per LSEG data — a direct hit to long-duration, high-multiple growth. Second, mounting doubt over hyperscaler AI capex: Amazon, Microsoft, Alphabet and Meta are planning roughly $600-700B of AI capex in 2026 (a 36-71% jump), and investors are demanding evidence the spend converts to profit. The combination re-rated the AI complex from momentum to a valuation-and-rates question.
3. The global leg — KOSPI -10%, Samsung & SK Hynix -12%, Nikkei -3.55%. The AI-valuation scare went global overnight into Tuesday. South Korea's KOSPI plummeted 9.99% as overseas investors dumped chip stocks, with bellwethers Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix each down more than 12%; Tokyo's Nikkei 225 fell 3.55%, snapping eight straight advances. The Korea tape is the direct read-through for SKM and a sentiment input for the whole memory cohort — a continued de-rate there pressures the book's Korea-sensitive leg most.
4. SK Hynix slows HBM4 to chase commodity DRAM — the fundamental hook. The week's most consequential supply signal: SK Hynix — the HBM market leader, with its 2026 HBM already sold out — is deliberately slowing its next-gen HBM4 ramp to redirect capacity into conventional DRAM, where acute shortages have made margins surprisingly attractive. The tape read it two ways at once: bearish, as a sign AI-compute demand may be cresting (why pivot away from HBM if demand were insatiable?), and bullish, for commodity-DRAM pricing tightness. This week the demand-cresting read dominated — the hook that turned a momentum unwind into a fundamental one.
Framework read: the controlling variable this week sharpened from a broad 'AI-valuation scare' into a specific AI-compute-demand question — SK Hynix's HBM4-to-DRAM pivot handed the bears a fundamental hook, not just a momentum one. For the book that keeps the bifurcation intact but raises the stakes on the memory legs: DRAM and SKM sit at the exact epicenter with the Micron print the decider, APLD takes the sentiment de-rate with its $31B take-or-pay book the floor, RDW rides its own catalyst path without the macro bid, and NOK — value/networking — is on the right side of the defensive rotation. The two events that resolve it both sit ahead: the Micron print after today's close and May PCE at 8:30 ET tomorrow.

Glossary

GEX (Gamma Exposure)
Net dealer gamma position. Positive = dealers stabilize (sell rallies, buy dips). Negative = amplify. Read qualitatively here via VIX term structure.
VIX9D
9-day implied vol on SPX. Captures the event window tighter than spot VIX (30-day). Compression vs spot = market pricing a binary.
Vanna
Sensitivity of option delta to IV. IV crush on good news forces mechanical dealer buying — "vol crush → mechanical bid."
Charm
Delta decay over time. Runs 24/7. Bleeds a call-heavy book even in a flat tape.
Magnet / Accelerator / Trapdoor
Strike vocabulary. Magnet = positive-gamma strike attracting price. Accelerator = negative-gamma strike amplifying. Trapdoor = cluster that cascades.
Contango / Backwardation
VIX curve shape. Contango (front < back) = normal. Backwardation (front > back) = stress.
MOVE
Bond-market VIX. Rising MOVE = rates vol expanding, cross-asset hedge pressure.
OVX
Oil-market VIX. Leading indicator for geopolitical repricing.
Binary
A known-date catalyst with two discrete outcomes. Requires different positioning than a trend.
