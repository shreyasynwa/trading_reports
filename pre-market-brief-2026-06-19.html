Wednesday's FOMC set the regime — a 12-0 hold but a sharply hawkish dot plot flags a 2026 rate hike The controlling event going into Monday is Wednesday's FOMC: in Kevin Warsh's first meeting the Fed held the funds rate 12-0 at 3.50-3.75%, but the Summary of Economic Projections turned sharply hawkish. The median dot now sees end-2026 funds at 3.8% — up from 3.4% in March and a quarter-point above the current range, implying a hike — with 9 of 18 members projecting at least one 2026 hike (6 favor two) and only one seeing a cut. Year-end PCE inflation was lifted to 3.6% (from 2.7%) and unemployment trimmed to 4.3%. Warsh declined to submit a personal dot and revamped the policy statement ('a bit shorter, a bit simpler'). The hawkish SEP drove Wednesday's selloff — the worst Fed-day for a new chair since 1994 — and keeps the regime hawkish-Fed-gated into Monday: the new controlling variable is the 2026 rate-hike bias, not the (now-resolved) geopolitical tape.
4. Oil keeps unwinding the war premium — WTI ~$75-77 as tankers exit a reopening Hormuz The bounce's macro tailwind is the energy complex. WTI crude trades ~$75-77 — near its lowest since early March — after the U.S. and Iran signed their Memorandum of Understanding in France; tankers carrying previously stranded crude began exiting the Strait of Hormuz on Thursday and Kuwait said it would begin raising production. Oil has now erased nearly all of the gains recorded since the Middle East conflict began in late February. The mechanical significance is large: the May inflation spike (CPI +4.2% y/y, PPI +6.5% y/y) was overwhelmingly energy-driven by the Hormuz closure, so a sustained oil decline pulls headline inflation lower into June/July — the central bull case that the hawkish dots overstate the inflation path and the Fed looks through the spike. The cross-current: lower oil is disinflationary (rate-relief positive) but the Fed's dots already price a 2026 hike, the offsetting hawkish anchor into Monday.
Framework read: A bounce that reclaims much of a Fed-day plunge is a classic relief retrace — it relieves the oversold condition without resolving the new overhang. The regime flipped from war-gated (May/early June) to Fed-gated (Monday's peace deal) and now specifically to hawkish-Fed-gated (Wednesday's dot plot): the 2026 rate-hike bias is the controlling variable, and the long-duration cohort (DRAM/APLD/SKM/NOK) carries the discount-rate drag it implies. The constructive distinction holds: the cohort is long-DTE and structurally insulated, the hawkish dots are a slow drag rather than a trigger, and the collapsing oil/energy impulse (WTI ~$75-77) is the mechanical bull case for the Fed eventually softening the dots. With the June-18 legs rolled off, the book is clean into Monday, and the three-day-weekend gap is the first test of whether the bounce holds.

Glossary

GEX (Gamma Exposure)
Net dealer gamma position. Positive = dealers stabilize (sell rallies, buy dips). Negative = amplify. Read qualitatively here via VIX term structure.
VIX9D
9-day implied vol on SPX. Captures the event window tighter than spot VIX (30-day). Compression vs spot = market pricing a binary.
Vanna
Sensitivity of option delta to IV. IV crush on good news forces mechanical dealer buying — "vol crush → mechanical bid."
Charm
Delta decay over time. Runs 24/7. Bleeds a call-heavy book even in a flat tape.
Magnet / Accelerator / Trapdoor
Strike vocabulary. Magnet = positive-gamma strike attracting price. Accelerator = negative-gamma strike amplifying. Trapdoor = cluster that cascades.
Contango / Backwardation
VIX curve shape. Contango (front < back) = normal. Backwardation (front > back) = stress.
MOVE
Bond-market VIX. Rising MOVE = rates vol expanding, cross-asset hedge pressure.
OVX
Oil-market VIX. Leading indicator for geopolitical repricing.
Binary
A known-date catalyst with two discrete outcomes. Requires different positioning than a trend.
