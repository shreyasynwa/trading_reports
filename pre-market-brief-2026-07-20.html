Historical perspective: The instructive pattern is not the original DeepSeek shock but what typically follows any sentiment shock with a dated fundamental answer behind it: price reliably repairs before information arrives, because positioning mean-reverts on a faster clock than fundamentals resolve. Oversold complexes bounce two to four sessions after a capitulation day regardless of whether the underlying question has been settled, and the bounce feels like confirmation precisely when it is only mechanics. What distinguishes the useful cases from the traps is whether the whole complex participates or whether a specific sub-sector stays marked down — a divergence like Synopsys's non-participation is historically the more informative signal, because it says the market made a considered judgement rather than a wholesale one. The second pattern worth respecting is that vol compressing into a known event has repeatedly produced outsized gaps, since the hedging that would have dampened the move was allowed to decay in the calm beforehand. Glossary

GEX (Gamma Exposure)
Net dealer gamma position. Positive = dealers stabilize (sell rallies, buy dips). Negative = amplify. Read qualitatively here via VIX term structure.
VIX9D
9-day implied vol on SPX. Captures the event window tighter than spot VIX (30-day). Compression vs spot = market pricing a binary.
Vanna
Sensitivity of option delta to IV. IV crush on good news forces mechanical dealer buying — "vol crush → mechanical bid."
Charm
Delta decay over time. Runs 24/7. Bleeds a call-heavy book even in a flat tape.
Magnet / Accelerator / Trapdoor
Strike vocabulary. Magnet = positive-gamma strike attracting price. Accelerator = negative-gamma strike amplifying. Trapdoor = cluster that cascades.
Contango / Backwardation
VIX curve shape. Contango (front < back) = normal. Backwardation (front > back) = stress.
MOVE
Bond-market VIX. Rising MOVE = rates vol expanding, cross-asset hedge pressure.
OVX
Oil-market VIX. Leading indicator for geopolitical repricing.
Binary
A known-date catalyst with two discrete outcomes. Requires different positioning than a trend.
