Quick Reference — Key Terms

GEX (Gamma Exposure)
Net dealer gamma position. Positive = stabilizing (sell rallies, buy dips); negative = amplifying. Approximated here qualitatively from VIX term structure since paid GEX feeds are not available.
VIX9D
9-day implied vol on SPX. Captures event windows tighter than spot VIX (30-day). VIX9D > spot VIX = front-end event premium.
Vanna
Sensitivity of option delta to IV. IV crush on benign news forces mechanical dealer buying.
Charm
Delta decay over time. Bleeds a call-heavy book even in a flat tape; runs 24/7 and accelerates into expiration.
Magnet / Accelerator / Trapdoor
Strike vocabulary. Magnet = positive-gamma strike attracting price. Accelerator = negative-gamma strike amplifying. Trapdoor = cluster that cascades.
Block tape
Institutional flow read. Approximated here via top movers since paid block-flow data is not available.

Bottom Line — Two Stories in One Session

The headline story: First digest day of the historic April semis run. SOXX gave back 2.4% after a 27.7% month. The trade isn't broken — Intel's Friday detonation showed the data center demand story is real — but positioning got crowded, and hedge fund cuts today are the natural exhale. Iran/oil added a geopolitical wedge: WTI $96, OVX 75.83. Equities took the path of least resistance lower (-0.2 to -0.4%) but stayed orderly. The defining feature was the absence of panic: VIX only +1.9%, term structure still shallow contango. Market is selling event vol into Wednesday, not buying protection.

The under-the-hood story: Three things matter under the hood. First, dealers are still long gamma in SPX 710–715 — that's why today's pullback was orderly and bought near 712. Mechanical buyers and sellers are damping range. Second, OVX/MOVE divergence: oil vol bid (Iran) but bond vol calm. That tells you this risk-off is geopolitical, not a rates/macro re-rating. If MOVE stays calm through FOMC, the equity damage is capped. Third, VIX9D < spot VIX means the market priced FOMC + Mag-7 as a binary that's already in spot vol but not extending into the longer expiries. Vol crush risk on benign prints is enormous — that's the right asymmetry to be on.

Mechanically honest read: SPY 715 is the line for the trend (broke today). 710 is the dealer pivot. 705 is the trapdoor — below that, gamma flip and the April rally hands back its top decile. The honest read is mechanical: dealers can't hedge a Wed FOMC + 4-print Mag-7 combo cleanly, so they're letting the tape wander in 710–715 with light hedging. A clean Mag-7 night = mechanical bid into Thursday on vol crush + delta buying. A bad Mag-7 night = gamma flip and the SOX overhang accelerates lower. The trade isn't bullish or bearish — it's asymmetric: own short-dated calls into vol crush, or own 705 puts as cheap insurance, but don't fight the dealer book in the middle.

Into Wed close: SPY 710 is the line. Holding it through FOMC = compressed tape resumes; mid-week vol crush mechanics dominate. Losing 710 with conviction = gamma flip → 705 magnet → forced derisking from CTAs and vol-targeted funds.
