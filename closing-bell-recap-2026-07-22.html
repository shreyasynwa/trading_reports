Quick Reference — Key Terms

GEX (Gamma Exposure)
Net dealer gamma position. Positive = stabilizing (sell rallies, buy dips); negative = amplifying. Approximated here qualitatively from VIX term structure since paid GEX feeds are not available.
VIX9D
9-day implied vol on SPX. Captures event windows tighter than spot VIX (30-day). VIX9D > spot VIX = front-end event premium.
Vanna
Sensitivity of option delta to IV. IV crush on benign news forces mechanical dealer buying.
Charm
Delta decay over time. Bleeds a call-heavy book even in a flat tape; runs 24/7 and accelerates into expiration.
Magnet / Accelerator / Trapdoor
Strike vocabulary. Magnet = positive-gamma strike attracting price. Accelerator = negative-gamma strike amplifying. Trapdoor = cluster that cascades.
Block tape
Institutional flow read. Approximated here via top movers since paid block-flow data is not available.

Bottom Line — Two Stories in One Session

Then the verdict: Alphabet beat on every line - revenue $119.8B (+24%), Cloud $24.77B (+82%) - but raised 2026 capex to $195-205B and fell ~5% AH as the market priced ROI discipline over demand; Tesla fell ~3% on an auto-margin miss (16.3% vs 18.4%) despite record 480,126 deliveries; Texas Instruments beat and raised with data-center revenue doubling YoY but drew a lukewarm tape. Underneath, oil extended and the 10Y held a two-month 4.64% high for a third session. The book's demand thesis was confirmed; the market's mood was not.

The under-the-hood story: Under the hood, the demand signal and the sentiment signal split cleanly - and that split is the whole story into Thursday. Alphabet raising capex to $205B and TXN's data-center revenue doubling are exactly what the book's AI-infra legs (DRAM, APLD, SKM) want to see: the build is accelerating, not slowing. But the market punished Alphabet ~5% for the very spending that constitutes that demand, and Tesla's margin miss reinforced a 'spending is now scrutinised' theme. So the fundamental case for the AI-infra suppliers strengthened tonight while the sentiment case got riskier, all against a third-session two-month-high 10Y that quietly taxes the long-duration names. The framework's read: the demand question resolved in the book's favour, but the market is asking an ROI question it cannot answer quickly, and that gap - between a confirmed order signal and a souring multiple - is where the next 48 hours of risk lives.
