Overnight Developments

What changed overnight: the week's AI de-rate reignited and went global — an OpenAI IPO-delay report (spark: SpaceX's ~35% post-IPO slide) reopened the AI-valuation question and tripped an 8% KOSPI circuit breaker, while Apple/Microsoft price hikes confirmed the memory crunch even as the tape sold AI; the selling is valuation/financing/positioning, not a memory-demand break.

1. An OpenAI IPO-delay report reignites AI-valuation fear — SpaceX's post-IPO slide the spark. An NYT report that OpenAI is weighing pushing its IPO to 2027 set the overnight tone: advisers reportedly warned that tech-stock volatility undercuts retail demand at the ~$1T valuation CEO Sam Altman is targeting (versus a ~$730-850B private mark), presenting a choice between waiting for stability or accepting a lower mark. The spark was SpaceX's ~35% one-week post-IPO slide that erased ~$600B+ in value, souring the marquee-AI-IPO trade. The read-through is doubt about the AI build-out's financing and valuation runway — the news slammed SoftBank (~-12%, its OpenAI exposure the channel) and rippled across the AI-infrastructure complex into the U.S. open. It reopened the 'AI is over-valued' question that Thursday's Micron blowout had answered on fundamentals.
2. Apple and Microsoft RAISE hardware prices — the AI memory crunch hits consumers. Apple lifted Mac and iPad prices (MacBook Air $1,099->$1,299, MacBook Pro $1,699->$1,999, iPad Air $599->$749) and Microsoft followed on Xbox (+$100-150), both citing surging memory/storage costs driven by AI-data-center demand — which dragged Apple -6% on Thursday. This is the AI memory crunch passing through to consumer prices. The crucial counter-read for the memory-supply side: Apple and Microsoft are raising prices because memory is scarce and getting scarcer — direct, real-world confirmation of supply tightness and pricing power for the memory makers, even as it's a margin negative for the device makers and a fresh bear hook for the AI-cost narrative.
4. U.S. futures point lower; chips lead the premarket weakness. S&P futures ~-0.5%, Nasdaq-100 futures ~-1.3%, Dow futures ~-94pts (-0.2%). Premarket single-names skew hard to chips and memory-adjacent names: ON Semiconductor -15%, SanDisk -5.4%, Avery Dennison -6.1%, with Micron and Western Digital soft and the Roundhill Mag-7 ETF (MAGS) lower. The breadth of the chip weakness signals an indiscriminate de-risk rather than a name-specific story — the kind of tape where good fundamentals (Micron) get sold alongside the valuation-exposed AI names.
5. Cross-asset confirms the de-risk — VIX bid near 20, dollar at a 1yr high, gold heavy, oil a crosscurrent. The VIX is bid near 20 (tech-vol running hotter than broad-vol — investors specifically hedging a tech-led shakeout); the 10Y sits ~4.45-4.49%; the dollar pushed to its highest in more than a year; and gold is heavy near $4,000 (down ~5% on the week on a hawkish-Fed residue). Oil is a crosscurrent — renewed Strait-of-Hormuz/Gulf-of-Oman disruption headlines against reports of tankers exiting the strait leave crude's direction unsettled into the open. The strong-dollar, hawkish-residue backdrop is a long-duration headwind for the high-multiple AI-capex cohort.
Framework read: the controlling variable this week has flipped from demand to VALUATION/FINANCING. The bears own three hooks — OpenAI's IPO/valuation doubt (SpaceX the spark), the cost narrative around Apple/MSFT price hikes, and the strong-dollar/hawkish residue — and the tape is selling the entire AI complex on them. But none is a memory-demand break; Micron just printed a sold-out blowout, and the Apple/MSFT price hikes confirm memory pricing power. For the book that splits the cohort: APLD sits most directly in the AI-IPO/data-center-doubt crosshairs (ballasted by its $31B take-or-pay book), DRAM/SKM ride memory beta down on the tape while their supply thesis is confirmed by the same news flow, and NOK/RDW are the relative shelters via the rotation-out-of-AI. The risks are tape/beta and discount-rate, not thesis-level — and the long DTE means the drag is marked, not triggered.

Glossary

GEX (Gamma Exposure)
Net dealer gamma position. Positive = dealers stabilize (sell rallies, buy dips). Negative = amplify. Read qualitatively here via VIX term structure.
VIX9D
9-day implied vol on SPX. Captures the event window tighter than spot VIX (30-day). Compression vs spot = market pricing a binary.
Vanna
Sensitivity of option delta to IV. IV crush on good news forces mechanical dealer buying — "vol crush → mechanical bid."
Charm
Delta decay over time. Runs 24/7. Bleeds a call-heavy book even in a flat tape.
Magnet / Accelerator / Trapdoor
Strike vocabulary. Magnet = positive-gamma strike attracting price. Accelerator = negative-gamma strike amplifying. Trapdoor = cluster that cascades.
Contango / Backwardation
VIX curve shape. Contango (front < back) = normal. Backwardation (front > back) = stress.
MOVE
Bond-market VIX. Rising MOVE = rates vol expanding, cross-asset hedge pressure.
OVX
Oil-market VIX. Leading indicator for geopolitical repricing.
Binary
A known-date catalyst with two discrete outcomes. Requires different positioning than a trend.
