Quick Reference — Key Terms

GEX (Gamma Exposure)
Net dealer gamma position. Positive = stabilizing (sell rallies, buy dips); negative = amplifying. Approximated here qualitatively from VIX term structure since paid GEX feeds are not available.
VIX9D
9-day implied vol on SPX. Captures event windows tighter than spot VIX (30-day). VIX9D > spot VIX = front-end event premium.
Vanna
Sensitivity of option delta to IV. IV crush on benign news forces mechanical dealer buying.
Charm
Delta decay over time. Bleeds a call-heavy book even in a flat tape; runs 24/7 and accelerates into expiration.
Magnet / Accelerator / Trapdoor
Strike vocabulary. Magnet = positive-gamma strike attracting price. Accelerator = negative-gamma strike amplifying. Trapdoor = cluster that cascades.
Block tape
Institutional flow read. Approximated here via top movers since paid block-flow data is not available.

The story into next week is whether this is a scare or a genuine de-rate — a question only the mega-cap capex guides (GOOGL 7/22; MSFT/META + FOMC 7/29; AAPL/AMZN 7/30) can answer.

The under-the-hood story: Under the hood, the binding constraint completed its shift from 'is demand real' to 'is the spend justified.' Earlier this week the AI-capex debate was about whether demand supported the spend (TSMC's raised capex said yes); the DeepSeek in-house-chip report and Kimi K3 reframed it as whether a near-frontier open model at ~$3/$15 per M tokens and hyperscaler-adjacent in-house silicon compress the pricing power and demand that fund that spend. That is a deeper, valuation-and-demand-level question than Thursday's rotation, which is why Friday's drag was broad (all indexes red) rather than an internal green-Dow rotation. The book's AI legs are pressured on the proxy but intact at the contract/cycle level — the real test is the fundamental verdict from the mega-cap guides, not today's sentiment repricing.
