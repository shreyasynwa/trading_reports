Overnight Developments

What changed into this open: both of the week's deciders cleared in the cohort's favor — Micron's print was a blowout that refuted the AI-demand fear, and May PCE landed the year's peak without an upside shock. The tape flipped from a fragile bounce inside an intact de-rate to a genuine risk-on reversal.

1. Micron blowout: ~$42B revenue, ~81% gross margin, $50B Q4 guide — the decider broke bullish. Micron reported fiscal Q3 after Tuesday's close and beat across the board: revenue near $42B (quadrupling from ~$9B a year earlier and crushing the ~$35B consensus), adjusted EPS above $25 (vs ~$21 expected), and a gross margin near 81%. The Q4 guide stunned — $50B +/- $1B against a ~$43B Street bar. Management said HBM4 12-high is ramping ~2x faster than HBM3E, it has already booked over $1B in HBM4 revenue, its 2026 HBM is sold out, and tightness is 'locked in to persist beyond calendar 2027' with the company able to fill only half-to-two-thirds of HBM demand. MU is up 12-18% pre-market, dragging the semis and the broader AI complex higher.
2. The Micron print refutes the SK Hynix 'demand-cresting' hook that drove the rout. Tuesday's two-day, semis-led drawdown rested on a single fundamental hook: SK Hynix slowing its HBM4 ramp to chase commodity-DRAM margins, read by the tape as a sign AI-compute demand might be cresting. Micron's print is the direct counter-evidence — the second-largest HBM supplier reporting it cannot make HBM fast enough, with tightness extending past 2027. The read-through reframes SK Hynix's pivot as exactly the commodity-DRAM tightness the bulls wanted (good for DRAM pricing) rather than a demand top. The memory complex was sold off into an accelerating up-cycle, not a peak.
3. May PCE printed the year's expected peak — but a tick below the feared headline. May PCE at 8:30 ET landed roughly as the year's peak inflation read but without the upside shock: headline +0.4% m/m / +4.1% y/y (the highest since April 2023, but below the +0.5% m/m economists feared), core +0.3% m/m / +3.4% y/y (highest since October 2023). The benign surprise eased the rate channel — the 10Y slipped toward ~4.45%, a 6-week low, and the dollar softened. The print neither validated nor escalated the BofA up-to-three-hikes path; it lifted the immediate macro overhang into the back half of the week.
4. Futures gap up, VIX crushes, cross-asset confirms the risk-on turn. Futures are gapping: ES ~+0.8%, NQ ~+2.3%, Dow ~+0.3%, Russell ~+0.7%. The VIX (~18.6 into the open) is compressing off its rout highs as the two binaries clear. Cross-asset confirms: the 10Y eased toward ~4.45% (lowest in 6+ weeks), WTI is back at pre-conflict levels (~$74) on US-Iran de-escalation progress, and the dollar softened on the benign PCE. The setup is a clean unwind of the two-day de-rate rather than a fragile bounce — the open's only real question is whether the gap holds.
Framework read: the controlling variable that broke the tape this week — the AI-compute-demand question — got answered by the demand side itself. Micron cannot make HBM fast enough and sees tightness past 2027, which converts the bears' fundamental hook back into a bull thesis and reframes SK Hynix's HBM4-to-DRAM pivot as commodity-DRAM tightness. With the second binary (May PCE) landing benign, the cohort opens on the right side of both: DRAM and SKM at a validated memory epicenter, APLD's lease-demand narrative underwritten, RDW with a marginal small-cap bid, NOK on the bull half of its networking thesis. The only live question is execution risk on the gap — whether a +2.3% Nasdaq open holds into the cash session or exhausts.

Glossary

GEX (Gamma Exposure)
Net dealer gamma position. Positive = dealers stabilize (sell rallies, buy dips). Negative = amplify. Read qualitatively here via VIX term structure.
VIX9D
9-day implied vol on SPX. Captures the event window tighter than spot VIX (30-day). Compression vs spot = market pricing a binary.
Vanna
Sensitivity of option delta to IV. IV crush on good news forces mechanical dealer buying — "vol crush → mechanical bid."
Charm
Delta decay over time. Runs 24/7. Bleeds a call-heavy book even in a flat tape.
Magnet / Accelerator / Trapdoor
Strike vocabulary. Magnet = positive-gamma strike attracting price. Accelerator = negative-gamma strike amplifying. Trapdoor = cluster that cascades.
Contango / Backwardation
VIX curve shape. Contango (front < back) = normal. Backwardation (front > back) = stress.
MOVE
Bond-market VIX. Rising MOVE = rates vol expanding, cross-asset hedge pressure.
OVX
Oil-market VIX. Leading indicator for geopolitical repricing.
Binary
A known-date catalyst with two discrete outcomes. Requires different positioning than a trend.
