Risk Matrix — Into the Open & Through the Session

1. Hot June NFP (150k+ / firm wages) re-runs the June-5 playbook into a one-session, no-tomorrow tape
High
The live precedent is a month old: May's +172k (vs 80k expected) took the Nasdaq down 4.2% and the SOX down 10% in a single session as hike odds spiked. Today's setup is more fragile in one way — the chip complex enters already down 10%+ on two sessions of de-grossing — and more dangerous in another: markets are closed Friday, so a hot print's repricing (hike path toward July-live, dollar/10Y re-armed) has one thin session to clear and a long weekend to stew. Position-book translation: DRAM/SKM take beta + rate channel (SKM adding the won drag), APLD/NOK bleed on duration. No trims fire on a macro repricing alone — no invalidation is touched. Counter-read: ADP +98k, consensus already ~100-115k, and Warsh declined to escalate — the setup tilts cooler.
2. The memory de-gross deepens into a genuine momentum unwind — day three with the base breaking
High
Two sessions of coordinated selling in the H1 champions (MU -10.6%, SNDK -10.6%, AMAT -10%) extending overnight is exactly what start-of-H2 institutional rebalancing looks like — until it isn't. The risk: the flush overwhelms the flow that was absorbing it, closing-basis bases break across the complex, and profit-taking becomes distribution. Position-book translation: the direct DRAM/SKM trigger-watch — a closing-basis base break is the armed line (the combination trigger), not intraday chop. Counter-read: the MU-GM long-term supply pact, HBM pre-sold through 2026, retail still bullish, and META's same-day +9% say demand is intact and this is positioning; the deeper the flush into intact tightness, the better the re-bid.
3. Long-weekend headline risk — Doha reversal or geopolitical flare with markets closed
Medium
The Doha round concluded 'going well,' but the ceasefire nearly broke last weekend (vessel attacks, retaliatory US strikes) — and the market is closed Friday through Sunday. A reversal headline lands on an untradeable tape and gaps Monday's open. Position-book translation: a re-escalation re-bids crude and the VIX against the whole cohort; RDW's defense premium is the partial offset. No trims fire on a geopolitical gap alone. Counter-read: crude's fourth straight weekly loss says the market is pricing sustained de-escalation, and both Trump and Vance are publicly invested in the talks succeeding.
4. TSLA deliveries miss the ~406k bar and sour the momentum tape into the close
Medium
With GS and Barclays publicly expecting a beat, the bar is set above consensus — a miss punishes a momentum cohort already de-grossing and hands the unwind narrative a second data point on the same day as the NFP reaction, in thin liquidity, into a long weekend. Position-book translation: no direct read-through; the channel is risk appetite spilling into the high-beta legs (DRAM/SKM most exposed). Counter-read: Europe/international strength underpins the beat case, and a beat is a same-day risk-appetite stabilizer after the chip flush.
5. The DRAM antitrust class action grows legs — regulatory follow-on against the memory complex
Low
The California suit alleging Samsung/SK Hynix/Micron coordinated DRAM supply (prices +~700% in four years) is, for now, a private class action — but pricing-power litigation against a complex whose entire bull case IS pricing power is a channel to respect. The risk is follow-on: a regulator opening an inquiry or discovery headlines that cap the multiple. Position-book translation: a DRAM/SKM sentiment overhang, not a thesis event — the tightness is real either way, and it is not the invalidation (a supply guide-down). Monitor; no action.

The asymmetry into the print is unusual: sentiment has already de-risked (F&G ~30 Fear, the chip complex down 10%+ in two sessions, retail the only bulls left in memory) while the demand evidence got stronger (MU-GM pact, META's AI-cloud entry) — so a cool NFP hits a flushed, defensively-positioned tape and can squeeze violently, while a hot print extends an unwind that is already partially cleared; the genuinely unpriced tail is a hot print PLUS a base break PLUS the Friday closure trapping the reaction, and none of it touches a thesis invalidation — the book's risk remains macro/positioning, marked not triggered, with no near-dated expiries.

Glossary

GEX (Gamma Exposure)
Net dealer gamma position. Positive = dealers stabilize (sell rallies, buy dips). Negative = amplify. Read qualitatively here via VIX term structure.
VIX9D
9-day implied vol on SPX. Captures the event window tighter than spot VIX (30-day). Compression vs spot = market pricing a binary.
Vanna
Sensitivity of option delta to IV. IV crush on good news forces mechanical dealer buying — "vol crush → mechanical bid."
Charm
Delta decay over time. Runs 24/7. Bleeds a call-heavy book even in a flat tape.
Magnet / Accelerator / Trapdoor
Strike vocabulary. Magnet = positive-gamma strike attracting price. Accelerator = negative-gamma strike amplifying. Trapdoor = cluster that cascades.
Contango / Backwardation
VIX curve shape. Contango (front < back) = normal. Backwardation (front > back) = stress.
MOVE
Bond-market VIX. Rising MOVE = rates vol expanding, cross-asset hedge pressure.
OVX
Oil-market VIX. Leading indicator for geopolitical repricing.
Binary
A known-date catalyst with two discrete outcomes. Requires different positioning than a trend.
