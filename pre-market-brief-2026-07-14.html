Risk Matrix — Into the Open & Through the Session

1. Core CPI comes in hot over $86 oil — the friendly headline is exposed as a June-dated mirage and the rate channel re-arms
High
The soft headline is built entirely on June's pre-escalation gasoline slide; this week's US-Iran Hormuz fire is a July story June's data can't see. If core prints firm (+0.3% m/m or a rounded 2.9%+), the market reads the negative headline as backward-looking and re-arms the rate channel from a 4.59% starting point — the duration-heavy AI cohort (APLD) breaks and the bank beats can't offset it, especially with Warsh live at 10 AM. Counter-read: core has been grinding lower, June energy was genuinely soft, and a rounded-cool core would powerfully validate the bull branch — the print is symmetric.
2. The US-Iran exchange escalates — Brent through $90 turns a war premium into a genuine supply shock
High
This is an active shooting conflict on the primary oil chokepoint: CENTCOM strikes, Iranian attacks on US Gulf bases, a claimed US blockade, Hormuz crossings collapsed to ~6/day. A tanker sinking, a base casualty event, or a formal closure pushes Brent from ~$86 toward $90+ and converts an inflation premium into a supply shock — re-pricing every rate-sensitive leg (APLD), taxing transports (UAL Wednesday), and forcing the Fed toward the hawkish tail. Counter-read: crude is still (thinly) flowing, the market has adapted to Hormuz risk repeatedly, and a de-escalation headline reverses the premium as fast as it built.
3. IBM's software/consulting de-rate metastasizes into the broad high-multiple complex
Med
IBM's miss already dragged NOW -7%, CRM -5%, ACN -8%, CTSH -7% pre-market. With the 10Y at 4.59%, a rate-sensitive growth cohort has no margin for a demand scare — if the IBM read is generalized to 'enterprise software spend is being cannibalized by AI hardware,' the de-rate broadens beyond the direct comps into the whole long-duration multiple. Counter-read: IBM's issue is company-specific execution (large deals slipping) and the capex it lost went to a bid part of the market (memory/servers) — a rotation within tech, not a demand collapse across it.
4. Warsh out-hawks expectations at 10 AM — the hike bias hardens two weeks before the FOMC
Med
A first testimony that explicitly endorses holding/hiking, or frames AI/energy as inflationary, would harden the rate path just as CPI lands and ~two weeks before the 7/28-29 FOMC — capping every risk rally regardless of earnings quality and keeping the 10Y bid. Counter-read: a new chair on his first testimony week has every incentive to preserve optionality, not pre-commit; the base case is continued non-committal hawkishness that changes little from the pre-print pricing.
5. 'Good news is no news' — bank blowouts that sell off signal a discounted, fear-of-heights market
Med
JPM posted one of its largest beats in memory (equities trading +86%) and trades -2%; WFC beat and trades -2%. When demonstrably strong prints can't lift their own stocks, the market is telling you the good news is fully discounted and the marginal buyer is exhausted — the next directional move is set by the un-discounted variable (rates/oil), not by more good earnings. Counter-read: sell-the-news on banks is a well-worn seasonal pattern, the credit commentary is a genuine floor, and one faded print is a positioning event, not a thesis break.

The skew into the open is asymmetric to the downside on the index but asymmetric to the upside for the book's core: a hot core or a $90 oil print breaks the rate-sensitive tape from a complacent VIX-15 base, yet the single most book-relevant signal — IBM confirming capex is flooding into supply-constrained memory — is a structural tailwind for DRAM/APLD that a soft macro print would amplify and a hot one would only defer, not negate.

Glossary

GEX (Gamma Exposure)
Net dealer gamma position. Positive = dealers stabilize (sell rallies, buy dips). Negative = amplify. Read qualitatively here via VIX term structure.
VIX9D
9-day implied vol on SPX. Captures the event window tighter than spot VIX (30-day). Compression vs spot = market pricing a binary.
Vanna
Sensitivity of option delta to IV. IV crush on good news forces mechanical dealer buying — "vol crush → mechanical bid."
Charm
Delta decay over time. Runs 24/7. Bleeds a call-heavy book even in a flat tape.
Magnet / Accelerator / Trapdoor
Strike vocabulary. Magnet = positive-gamma strike attracting price. Accelerator = negative-gamma strike amplifying. Trapdoor = cluster that cascades.
Contango / Backwardation
VIX curve shape. Contango (front < back) = normal. Backwardation (front > back) = stress.
MOVE
Bond-market VIX. Rising MOVE = rates vol expanding, cross-asset hedge pressure.
OVX
Oil-market VIX. Leading indicator for geopolitical repricing.
Binary
A known-date catalyst with two discrete outcomes. Requires different positioning than a trend.
