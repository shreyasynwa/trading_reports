Quick Reference — Key Terms

GEX (Gamma Exposure)
Net dealer gamma position. Positive = stabilizing (sell rallies, buy dips); negative = amplifying. Approximated here qualitatively from VIX term structure since paid GEX feeds are not available.
VIX9D
9-day implied vol on SPX. Captures event windows tighter than spot VIX (30-day). VIX9D > spot VIX = front-end event premium.
Vanna
Sensitivity of option delta to IV. IV crush on benign news forces mechanical dealer buying.
Charm
Delta decay over time. Bleeds a call-heavy book even in a flat tape; runs 24/7 and accelerates into expiration.
Magnet / Accelerator / Trapdoor
Strike vocabulary. Magnet = positive-gamma strike attracting price. Accelerator = negative-gamma strike amplifying. Trapdoor = cluster that cascades.
Block tape
Institutional flow read. Approximated here via top movers since paid block-flow data is not available.

The under-the-hood story: The book is saying something the headline isn't: three of eleven sectors closed red, defensives and cable-broadband were dumped, and VXN refused to follow VIX lower because the options market knows the next four trading sessions contain FOMC, GDP, PCE, ECI, and five mega-cap prints. The breadth weakness isn't a deteriorating tape — it's positioning compression ahead of the catalyst wall. Real money is sitting on hands; semis ran on a single fundamental print plus short-cover.

Mechanically honest read: SPX/Nasdaq record close on a narrow trend day is structurally bullish until proven otherwise — the call wall above (SPY 715/720) is intact and the bid was orderly. But the index is dependent on semi follow-through Monday and capex confirmation Wednesday. Two failure modes: (1) SOX day-19 fades, INTC mean-reverts to +12-15% off this gap, XLK gives back 100-150 bps, SPY tests 710; (2) one of GOOGL/MSFT/META capex guides disappoints Wed PM and the entire AI-infra cycle thesis re-rates lower in 30 minutes.

Into Monday's open: SPY 715.50 is the line. Holding it through 11:00 ET = trend day extension, semis take the cycle bid into mega-cap prints.
