5. OER distortion misread — market buys relief on 'contained core' then realizes it was noise
🟡 TECHNICAL RISK
The one-time OER catch-up from the October shutdown could mask genuine underlying core reacceleration. If core prints 2.7% but the OER adjustment accounts for 0.10-0.15% of that, the 'true' underlying core run rate is closer to 2.55-2.60% — which is actually benign. Conversely, if the market prices a 2.7% core print as 'clean and contained,' then next month when the OER distortion fades and energy spills further into core services, May CPI surprises to the upside again. The risk is a whipsaw: relief rally today on in-line CPI, then renewed pressure in 4-6 weeks as the true underlying trend re-asserts.

The asymmetric leg is to the upside on an in-line CPI: a hot print merely confirms what FedWatch already prices (no cuts in 2026), while a cool print (≤3.5%) would be a genuine positive surprise that unlocks a re-rating of the entire growth book — the market is structurally positioned for bad news, making good news the higher-volatility outcome.

Glossary

GEX (Gamma Exposure)
Net dealer gamma position. Positive = dealers stabilize (sell rallies, buy dips). Negative = amplify. Read qualitatively here via VIX term structure.
VIX9D
9-day implied vol on SPX. Captures the event window tighter than spot VIX (30-day). Compression vs spot = market pricing a binary.
Vanna
Sensitivity of option delta to IV. IV crush on good news forces mechanical dealer buying — "vol crush → mechanical bid."
Charm
Delta decay over time. Runs 24/7. Bleeds a call-heavy book even in a flat tape.
Magnet / Accelerator / Trapdoor
Strike vocabulary. Magnet = positive-gamma strike attracting price. Accelerator = negative-gamma strike amplifying. Trapdoor = cluster that cascades.
Contango / Backwardation
VIX curve shape. Contango (front < back) = normal. Backwardation (front > back) = stress.
MOVE
Bond-market VIX. Rising MOVE = rates vol expanding, cross-asset hedge pressure.
OVX
Oil-market VIX. Leading indicator for geopolitical repricing.
Binary
A known-date catalyst with two discrete outcomes. Requires different positioning than a trend.
